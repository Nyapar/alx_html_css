Holberton School

↩

About Holberton School

↩

Holberton School is a school that offers intensive training in computer science.

↩

The school was founded in 2013 by Julien Barbier and Sylvain Kalache.

↩

Holberton School has campuses in San Francisco, California, and New York City, New York.

↩

Curriculum

↩

Holberton School's curriculum is based on the principles of project-based learning.

↩

Students learn by working on real-world projects, such as building a web application or a mobile app.

↩

Holberton School's curriculum is also designed to be rigorous and challenging.

↩

Admissions

↩

Holberton School's admissions process is competitive.

↩

Students must apply and be accepted to the school.

↩

Holberton School also offers a scholarship program for students who are unable to afford the tuition.

↩

Alumni

↩

Holberton School has a number of successful alumni.

↩

Some of Holberton School's alumni have gone on to work at companies such as Google, Facebook, and Amazon.

↩

Holberton School's alumni have also started their own successful companies.
